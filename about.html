substance

Robotics is a multidisciplinary science that deals with the design,
production and use of robots. It is a common field of study of mechanical engineering,
aeronautical engineering,[1] aerospace engineering,[2] electronic engineering, computer engineering,
mechatronics engineering and control engineering. Robots are complex machines that are managed through software
and produce work and value for a useful purpose. Robotics technology aims to build machines that can replace
humans or mimic human actions. Although robots are intended to be used in many different situations, today they are
mostly used in hazardous environments[3] (e.g. bomb disposal[4]), production processes,[5] or in space,
underwater, high temperature and radiation environments where humans
cannot live. Although robots can be made in any form, some robots are made
to resemble humans. This is thought to facilitate the acceptance of robots by humans. Many robots are inspired by
nature and are the subject of bio-inspired robotics.[3] Translated with www.DeepL.com/Translator (free version)
