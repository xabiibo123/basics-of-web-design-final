The term robot comes from the Slavic word "robota" meaning labor or drudgery. The term "robot" as a term for humanoid machines first appeared in Czech author Karel Čapek's (1890–1938) play Rossums Universal Robots. Isaac Asimov, a science fiction writer, first mentioned robots in 1942 in his short story “Runaround”. However, the desire and idea of ​​producing automatic machines goes back much further. Leonardo Da Vinci made sketches and plans for robot construction. Humans have always been fascinated by creating machines that can perform or interact with tasks. According to Thomas Christaller, robots are "sensor-powered machines that allow to expand human capacity." Robots are made up of mechatronic components, sensors, and computer-based control functions. Robotics is a multidisciplinary science that deals with the design, manufacture, and use of robots. Although robots are intended to be used in many different situations, today they are mostly used in dangerous missions, in space, underwater, high temperature and radiation environments where human beings cannot live. Robots can be made in any shape, and some robots are made to be human-like. It is thought that this will facilitate the acceptance of robots by humans. What Are the Main Components of the Robot?
